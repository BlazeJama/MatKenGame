# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students and educators studying military vehicles — history, military science, defence studies, or personal interest. Users approach with varying prior knowledge; many start from curiosity and return to beat their own score. Sessions are short (one 10-question round), often squeezed into study time on a phone or laptop. The fun facts matter as much as the result — the quiz is a mechanism for learning, not just scoring.

## Product Purpose

MatKenGame is a photograph-based military vehicle recognition quiz. A player sees a vehicle photo, chooses from four options (name + country), gets instant feedback, and learns a fact about the vehicle. A round is 10 questions; the final score reflects how much the player already knows and motivates them to close the gap. Success means a player who can identify more vehicles after a session than before.

## Brand Personality

Educational, credible, purposeful. "Learn through challenge." The interface is a teaching tool that creates appropriate tension to deepen retention — not a reward shower, not a stress test.

## Anti-references

- **Kahoot / Quizlet / generic quiz apps**: pastel bubbles, oversized smiley feedback, deliberately childlike or cheerful presentation. MatKenGame deals with serious subject matter and should feel correspondingly grown-up and focused.
- **Mobile free-to-play games**: notification storms, gem economies, aggressive reward animations, FOMO mechanics. This is a clean educational tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Information before spectacle.** The vehicle photograph, its name, and the fun fact are the product. Every UI element exists to serve them, not to compete.
2. **Tension aids retention.** The quiz format creates cognitive investment. The design should sustain focus without tipping into anxiety — pressure is productive, stress is not.
3. **Feedback is a teaching moment.** Correct and wrong states should feel like discovery, not judgment. The fun fact panel is as important as the answer highlight.
4. **Earned aesthetic.** The tactical visual language earns its character through direct relevance to military subject matter. It is not decoration — it contextualises the content.
5. **Clarity at every size.** Students reach for their phones during study sessions. Small-screen legibility is non-negotiable, especially for vehicle names, country labels, and fun-fact text.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. Key areas to monitor given the dark theme:
- Amber (`#f59e0b`) on near-black (`#070b14`) — verify contrast ratio meets 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large/bold.
- All answer button states (default, correct, wrong, dimmed) must maintain readable contrast.
- Keyboard navigation through quiz options must be fully supported.
- Reduced-motion preference: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for entry animations.
